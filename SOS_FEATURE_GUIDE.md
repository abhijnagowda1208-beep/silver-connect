# SOS (Emergency Alert) Feature - Complete Setup Guide

## Overview
The SOS feature allows you to send emergency alerts to your primary emergency contact with a single click. This is crucial for elderly users who need immediate help in emergencies.

---

## Step 1: Add Emergency Contacts

### How to Add a Contact:
1. Go to your **Profile**
2. Click on "Safety Network" section
3. Click the **"Manage"** button
4. Click **"+ Add New Contact"**
5. Fill in the contact details:
   - **Name**: Full name of the emergency contact
   - **Relationship**: Select from (Daughter, Son, Spouse, Doctor, Neighbor, Other)
   - **Phone Number**: Contact's phone number

### Supported Relationships:
- Daughter
- Son
- Spouse
- Doctor
- Neighbor
- Other

---

## Step 2: Set Primary Contact (IMPORTANT!)

The **Primary Contact** is the person who will receive SOS alerts.

### How to Set Primary Contact:
1. Go to **Profile** → **Safety Network** → **"Manage"**
2. In the contacts list, find the contact you want as primary
3. Click the **"Set as Primary"** button
4. This contact will now be marked with a red "SOS RECIPIENT" badge

**⚠️ Important:** You must have a primary contact set for SOS to work!

---

## Step 3: Trigger SOS Alert

### How to Send Emergency Alert:
1. Go to your **Dashboard**
2. Look for the red **"SOS"** card (usually in the bottom section)
3. Click the SOS button
4. Confirm the popup message: "Send SOS Alert?"
5. ✅ Your primary emergency contact will be notified immediately!

### What Happens When You Trigger SOS:
1. An alert is created in the system with timestamp
2. Your primary contact receives an alert notification
3. A message is logged in your SOS History
4. You'll see a confirmation message on the dashboard

---

## Step 4: View SOS History

### How to Check SOS Records:
1. Go to your **Profile**
2. In the "Safety Network" section, click **"History"**
3. You'll see:
   - Total number of SOS alerts triggered
   - List of all past alerts with:
     - Date and time triggered
     - Contact name who was notified
     - Contact relationship
     - Alert status (Sent, Pending, Failed)

---

## Technical Details

### SOS Alert Status:
- **SENT**: Alert was successfully sent to the primary contact
- **PENDING**: Alert is being processed
- **FAILED**: Alert could not be sent (may need contact verification)

### Data Logged:
When you trigger SOS, the system records:
- ✓ Timestamp (exact date and time)
- ✓ Your name and username
- ✓ Primary contact name and phone number
- ✓ Your address (if set in profile)
- ✓ Alert status
- ✓ Full alert message

### Admin View:
- Administrators can view all SOS alerts in the Admin Dashboard
- This helps monitor emergency events across the platform

## Troubleshooting

### Issue: SOS Button Not Working
**Solution:**
1. Ensure you've set a PRIMARY emergency contact
2. Go to Profile → Safety Network → Manage
3. Check that one contact is marked as "SOS RECIPIENT"

### Issue: "No primary contact set" Error
**Solution:**
1. Add an emergency contact first
2. Click "Set as Primary" to make it your active SOS contact
3. Try pressing SOS again

### Issue: How to Edit Contact Details
**Solution:**
1. Go to Profile → Safety Network → Manage
2. Click the contact's edit button
3. Update the information
4. Save changes

### Issue: Remove a Contact
**Solution:**
1. Go to Profile → Safety Network → Manage
2. Find the contact
3. Click the delete/trash button
4. Confirm deletion (Note: If this was your primary contact, you'll need to set a new one)

## Best Practices

✅ **Always keep your primary contact updated**
- Update phone numbers if they change
- Keep your address information current for emergency responders

✅ **Test your setup**
- Add a contact
- Set as primary
- Verify the settings are saved

✅ **Inform your emergency contacts**
- Tell them they're your primary contact
- Explain that they may receive SOS alerts
- Ensure their phone number is always active

✅ **Use responsibly**
- Only trigger SOS in genuine emergency situations
